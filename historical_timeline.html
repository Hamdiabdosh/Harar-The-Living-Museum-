Harar's Historical Timeline

896 AD - Harar Founded

Sheikh Abadir establishes a Muslim sultanate.

A spiritual leader unites seven villages into a thriving Islamic hub.

Legend says… faith forged a legacy.

1270–1543 - Walasma/Ifat Wars

300 years of war with the Solomonic dynasty.

Harar battles Abyssinia, holding strong amid centuries of strife.

Legend says… swords clashed for centuries.

1529 - Adal Dynasty Peak

Imam Ahmed leads Adal to prominence.

A warrior imam ends wars, cementing Harar’s power.

Legend says… valor crowned the city.

1551/52 - Jugol Walls Built

Amir Nur constructs a mighty defensive wall.

After Imam Ahmed’s wars, Amir Nur’s 6666-cubit wall guards Harar.

Legend says… stone blessed by faith stood tall.

1887 - Annexation by Ethiopia

Menelik II annexes Harar after Chelenqo.

Ethiopia’s emperor ends Harar’s sovereignty in battle.

Legend says… history turned anew.

1887 - Rimbaud’s Stay

Arthur Rimbaud arrives as a trader.

A French poet trades in Harar, leaving a global mark.

Legend says… words wove a new tale.

Notable Figures of Harar

  • Abadir Umar ar-Rida (c. 896 AD) - Spiritual founder of Harar, uniting the region under Islam.
  • Ahmad ibn Ibrahim al-Ghazi (1527–1543) - Imam who led Adal’s conquests, strengthening Harar’s power.
  • Bati del Wambara (c. 1527–1559) - Wife of Imam Ahmed and later Amir Nur, key figure in Harar’s resilience.
  • Nur ibn Mujahid (1551/52–1568) - Built the Jugol Wall, fortifying Harar after years of war.
  • Ali ibn Da’ud (1647–1671) - Founded the Emirate of Harar, starting a new dynasty.
  • Abd Allah I ibn Ali (1671–1700) - Improved relations with Oromos through marriage alliances.
  • Abd al-Shakur ibn Yusuf (1783–1794) - Strengthened Harar’s trade amid regional shifts.
  • Abd Allah II ibn Ali (1884–1887) - Last Amir, defeated at Chelenqo, ending Harar’s independence.
